Simulations & Code

Throughout my PhD research, I have developed several computational frameworks and codes for water flow and solute transport in porous media.

Computational Development

During my research, I have developed comprehensive codes for modeling water flow and solute transport in porous media, including fertilizer transport such as potassium, phosphorous, and nitrogen (urea, ammonium, and nitrate) with root water and nutrient uptake and other processes like hydrolysis, nitrification, denitrification which are biochemical reactions, and also evapotranspiration using implicit and semi-implicit finite element methods. My work spans from simple sequential codes to sophisticated parallel implementations:

Sequential Codes
  • FreeFem++
  • MATLAB
  • Python
  • Fortran
Parallel & GPU Codes
  • CUDA Fortran GPU acceleration
  • JAX Python parallel computing
  • High-performance implementations
  • Scalable algorithms for large systems
Key Physical & Biochemical Processes
  • Water flow in unsaturated porous media
  • Solute transport with advection-dispersion
  • Root water and nutrient uptake
  • Evapotranspiration processes
  • Hydrolysis of organic compounds
  • Nitrification (NH₄⁺ → NO₃⁻)
  • Denitrification biochemical reactions
  • Coupled transport phenomena
